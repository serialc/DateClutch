Information We Collect From You and How We Collect It

If you are filling out a poll, only your name is stored in the database and visible to the poll creators. Email addresses used for selection confirmation are not stored, but simply used to send the email. If you have a DateClutch account, then your email and username are stored in the database. Your email and name are shared with those that complete the polls you have created.

We may collect this information when you:

  • Complete a poll
  • Become a registered DateClutch user

Cookies and Similar Technologies

When you use the Services, we use cookies to identify you and manage your privileges on the Service. These are necessary to deliver the Service. No other cookies, by third parties are present on the platform. A cookie is tiny file that a website stores in a visitor’s browser, on their computer or device. The visitor’s browser provides this cookie to the website each time the visitor returns. DateClutch uses cookies to make the Services more easily navigable and useful for you.

How to Refuse the Use of Cookies

Most browsers include tools to help you manage cookies. For example, you should be able to choose to have your browser warn you each time a cookie is being sent, or you can choose to turn off (i.e., refuse to accept) all cookies. Each browser is different, however, so please consult your browser’s “Help” menu to learn the correct way to modify how your browser handles cookies. You can find more information about cookies and how to disable cookies at www.allaboutcookies.org.

Keep in mind that we need certain information in order for the Services to function properly. If you disable cookies, you may no longer be able to use or access some features of the Services.
